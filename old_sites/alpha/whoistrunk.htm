Who is Traveling Treasure Trunk?

 

   Started 13 years ago, Tufts Universityís Traveling Treasure Trunk remains a unique blend of theater performance and community service.  Trunk boasts itself as Tuftsí ONLY childrenís entertainment troupe, and takes tremendous pride in the quality of our performances. Our current troupe consists of 13 active members and 4 members abroad.

Trunk is proudly sponsored by both the Leonard Carmichael Society, which funds all Tufts Community Service Groups, and by Tufts theatre umbrella organization, Pen, Paint, and Pretzels (3Ps).

    The members of Traveling Treasure Trunk write or adapt our own plays and songs, then perform shows for Boston area schools, hospitals, libraries, and day-care centers. Normally, our performances are free of charge, but any donation we receive is given to local childrenís organizations.

  For the Spring 2001 Semester, Traveling Treasure Trunk is performing 3 of the following plays at each show.

Alibopolis Written by Alex Bush

               This immagitive show takes the children into the magical land of Alibopolis, a land where everything is made out of candy and it snows every day. However, we soon discover that the snow only began after Alibopolis had a run-in with the tangiest person in the world - KEY LIME PIE (and sidekick Cylinder Boy!)    

Cuando La Luna Bajo' a la Tierra As told by Carlos Genovese            ---> Adapted by Ariana Wohl

                A folk-tale from South America explaining the origin of mate (a bitter tea that is drunk throughout South America.) It all begins caundo la Luna bajo' a la tierra (when the moon comes down to earth) to experience a world different from her celestial home. The unique aspect of this play is the incorporation Spanish, along with English, in the dialogue. Students have the opportunty to learn some simple Spanish words while learning how the Moon, mate, and one corn tortilla are related.  

Its so Nice to have a Wolf around the House Written By Harry Allard    ---> Adapted by Alex Bush

                When an old man, an old dog, and a very old fish need some help around the house, who responds to there ad but Rupert P. Stratsbury. Rupert insists that, although he appears to be a wolf, he is actually a german shepard. The old family is happy to have this new, helpful companion around the house - until they find out that Rupert actually IS a wolf!

Sylvester and the Magic Pebble  Written by William Stieg                    ---> Adapted by Preetha Mani

                Sylvester is a donkey who loves collecting stones. One day, he find a magic pebble which grants all his wishes. However, the pebble grants Sylvester more than he wished for, leaving his parents wondering where Sylvester dissapeared to.
